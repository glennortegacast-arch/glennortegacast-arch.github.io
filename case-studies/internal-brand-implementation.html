01Context

A 1,500-person plant with no internal brand presence.

During a renovation of the plant's offices, I identified a gap: the facility had no internal corporate branding. There was no signage and no visual presence communicating the company's mission, vision, or values.

Across a site of 1,500 employees, this contributed to a visible disconnect between the workforce and the company they were part of.

The opportunity was clear — use branding to strengthen employees' sense of belonging, and reflect internally the same high quality standards the plant delivers in production.

02My Role

From global guideline to plant-floor reality.

Headquarters had defined the core brand system — color palette, photography, and GT America Extended as the corporate typeface. My job was to translate that global guideline into the physical and digital reality of the plant.

That meant interpreting the standards and applying them consistently across every internal touchpoint — from wayfinding to day-to-day communications — so the site spoke in one coherent voice.
